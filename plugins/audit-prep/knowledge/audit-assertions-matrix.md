# Audit Assertions Matrix

The five primary financial statement assertions, mapped to specific audit procedures for each major account area.

---

## The Five Assertions

| Assertion | Definition | Applies To |
|-----------|-----------|------------|
| **Existence / Occurrence** | Assets and liabilities exist at period end; transactions actually occurred | Balance sheet accounts / Income statement transactions |
| **Completeness** | All transactions and accounts that should be recorded are recorded | All accounts |
| **Valuation / Accuracy** | Assets, liabilities, revenue, and expenses are recorded at appropriate amounts | All accounts |
| **Rights and Obligations** | The entity has rights to assets and obligations for liabilities | Balance sheet accounts |
| **Presentation and Disclosure** | Accounts are properly classified, described, and disclosed | Financial statement presentation |

---

## Cash and Cash Equivalents

### Existence / Occurrence
- Obtain bank confirmations directly from financial institutions for all accounts
- Perform surprise or year-end petty cash count and reconcile to GL
- Inspect bank statements for the last month of the period
- Verify bank account ownership matches the entity name
- Test bank reconciliation items (outstanding checks, deposits in transit) by tracing to subsequent clearing

### Completeness
- Obtain management representation that all bank accounts have been disclosed
- Review prior year workpapers for accounts that may have been closed or opened
- Search for unrecorded accounts by reviewing interest income, bank fees, and intercompany transfers
- Confirm no accounts exist at banks where the company previously had relationships
- Inspect cash receipts and disbursements cutoff around year-end

### Valuation / Accuracy
- Recalculate bank reconciliations for all significant accounts
- Agree bank reconciliation balances to bank statements and general ledger
- Investigate all reconciling items over a defined threshold
- Test foreign currency translation for foreign-denominated accounts
- Verify money market and sweep accounts are properly valued at fair value

### Rights and Obligations
- Confirm through bank confirmations that accounts are not pledged as collateral
- Inquire of management about any restrictions on cash (compensating balances, escrow)
- Review loan agreements for cash sweep or lockbox requirements
- Verify authorized signers on all accounts match current management

### Presentation and Disclosure
- Verify restricted cash is separately presented on the balance sheet
- Ensure cash equivalents meet the definition (original maturity of 3 months or less)
- Confirm proper classification between current and non-current (if restricted)
- Review footnotes for disclosure of compensating balance arrangements

## Accounts Receivable

### Existence / Occurrence
- Send positive confirmations to a sample of customers (stratified by balance)
- For non-responses, perform alternative procedures: trace to subsequent cash receipts, match to shipping documents and invoices
- Vouch a sample of recorded sales near year-end to shipping documentation
- Inspect credit memos issued after year-end for returns related to pre-year-end sales
- Review AR aging for unusual items (credit balances, very old balances, related party balances)

### Completeness
- Perform sales cutoff testing: examine shipping logs 5 days before and after year-end, match to recorded revenue
- Reconcile AR subledger to general ledger
- Analyze unrecorded revenue by comparing shipping records to sales invoices
- Search for unrecorded credit memos by reviewing post-year-end adjustments
- Compare AR balances to prior periods and investigate significant fluctuations

### Valuation / Accuracy
- Test the allowance for doubtful accounts methodology and recalculate
- Age the receivables independently and compare to client aging
- Review subsequent collections on large past-due balances
- Evaluate specific reserves for known disputed or doubtful accounts
- Assess reasonableness of the allowance as a percentage of total AR and compare to prior years and industry

### Rights and Obligations
- Confirm through bank/lender confirmations whether AR has been factored or pledged
- Review financing arrangements for any assignment of receivables
- Inquire about bill-and-hold arrangements or consignment sales
- Verify amounts billed represent goods/services actually delivered (not advance billings)

### Presentation and Disclosure
- Verify trade receivables are separate from related party receivables, employee advances, and other receivables
- Ensure long-term receivables are classified as non-current
- Review disclosures for significant concentrations of credit risk
- Verify the allowance is presented as a contra-asset (not netted without disclosure)

## Inventory

### Existence / Occurrence
- Observe the physical inventory count (or cycle counts) at year-end
- Perform independent test counts during observation and compare to client counts
- Trace test count results to the final inventory compilation
- Inspect inventory for condition (damage, obsolescence)
- Confirm inventory held by third parties (warehouses, consignment) through direct confirmation

### Completeness
- Reconcile physical count to perpetual records and general ledger
- Perform cutoff testing on receiving reports and shipping logs around year-end
- Verify that goods in transit are properly included or excluded based on FOB terms
- Test for consignment inventory that should be excluded from the client's records
- Review subsequent period inventory adjustments for indications of incomplete year-end count

### Valuation / Accuracy
- Test inventory costing by vouching costs to vendor invoices (raw materials) or standard cost analysis (manufactured)
- Evaluate overhead allocation methodology for manufactured inventory
- Perform lower of cost or net realizable value (LCNRV) analysis
- Test the obsolescence reserve methodology and recalculate
- Compare gross margins by product line to prior periods for reasonableness
- Verify LIFO reserve calculation if LIFO method is used

### Rights and Obligations
- Verify the entity owns the inventory (not consignment or customer-owned materials)
- Confirm inventory is not pledged as collateral (or properly disclosed if it is)
- Review purchase agreements for any title transfer terms
- Inquire about goods held on behalf of customers

### Presentation and Disclosure
- Verify proper classification (raw materials, WIP, finished goods)
- Ensure the cost method is disclosed (FIFO, LIFO, weighted average)
- Review disclosures for pledged inventory
- Verify any significant write-downs are disclosed

## Fixed Assets (Property, Plant, and Equipment)

### Existence / Occurrence
- Physically inspect a sample of significant fixed asset additions
- Vouch additions to vendor invoices, contracts, and authorization documentation
- Verify disposals by inspecting sales agreements, scrap documentation, or insurance claims
- Trace a sample of assets from the fixed asset register to physical existence
- Review CIP projects for proper status (still in progress vs. should be placed in service)

### Completeness
- Review repair and maintenance expense for items that should have been capitalized
- Scan the general ledger for large charges to expense accounts that may represent capital items
- Compare additions to the capital budget for items that may not have been recorded
- Reconcile the fixed asset subledger to the general ledger
- Inquire about donated assets or assets acquired through non-cash transactions

### Valuation / Accuracy
- Recalculate depreciation expense for a sample of assets
- Verify useful lives and methods are consistent with policy and prior years
- Test the gain/loss calculation on asset disposals
- Evaluate impairment indicators and test for impairment if triggers exist
- Verify capitalization threshold is consistently applied
- Recalculate Section 179 and bonus depreciation for tax-basis reporting

### Rights and Obligations
- Inspect titles, deeds, and vehicle registrations for ownership
- Review lease agreements to determine proper classification (operating vs. finance)
- Confirm assets are not pledged as collateral (or properly disclosed)
- Verify that assets under construction are on the entity's property or under contract

### Presentation and Disclosure
- Verify proper classification by asset type (land, buildings, equipment, vehicles)
- Ensure accumulated depreciation is properly presented
- Review disclosures for depreciation methods, useful lives, and capitalization thresholds
- Verify finance lease right-of-use assets are separately presented

## Accounts Payable and Accrued Liabilities

### Existence / Occurrence
- Send confirmations to a sample of vendors (especially those with zero or small balances)
- Vouch a sample of recorded payables to vendor invoices and receiving reports
- Verify accrual calculations (payroll, vacation, bonuses) with supporting documentation
- Review post-year-end payments and credit memos for validity of year-end balances

### Completeness
- Perform a search for unrecorded liabilities: examine cash disbursements for 4-6 weeks after year-end
- Review vendor statements received after year-end and compare to recorded balances
- Examine receiving reports near year-end for goods received but not invoiced
- Analyze purchase commitments and open purchase orders at year-end
- Compare accrued liabilities to prior year and investigate missing or significantly reduced accruals
- Inquire of management and legal counsel about contingent liabilities

### Valuation / Accuracy
- Reconcile AP subledger to general ledger
- Age the payables and investigate unusually old items
- Recalculate significant accruals (payroll, vacation, warranty, bonus)
- Verify proper foreign currency translation of foreign-denominated payables
- Test mathematical accuracy of vendor invoices for a sample of large balances

### Rights and Obligations
- Verify that recorded liabilities represent actual obligations of the entity
- Review for liabilities that should be recorded by related entities
- Confirm personal obligations of owners are not recorded as company liabilities
- Verify proper classification of amounts owed to related parties

### Presentation and Disclosure
- Verify current vs. non-current classification
- Ensure related party payables are separately disclosed
- Review disclosures for significant purchase commitments
- Verify contingent liabilities are properly disclosed or accrued per ASC 450

## Revenue

### Existence / Occurrence
- Vouch a sample of recorded revenue transactions to source documents (contracts, invoices, shipping documents, proof of delivery)
- Test a sample of journal entries to revenue accounts for proper support
- Perform analytical procedures comparing revenue by month, product, and customer to prior periods
- Scan for unusual revenue entries (manual journal entries, round amounts, entries near period end)
- Confirm significant revenue transactions with customers

### Completeness
- Perform cutoff testing: trace shipping documents from before and after year-end to recorded revenue
- Reconcile revenue per the GL to revenue per sales system or billing system
- Analyze deferred revenue for proper recognition timing
- Review contracts for performance obligations that should trigger revenue recognition
- Test completeness of deferred revenue by examining cash receipts without corresponding revenue

### Valuation / Accuracy
- Recalculate revenue recognition for a sample of contracts under ASC 606 (identify performance obligations, determine transaction price, allocate, recognize)
- Test significant estimates in variable consideration (rebates, returns, penalties)
- Verify proper application of revenue recognition policy across all revenue streams
- Test mathematical accuracy of invoices
- Analyze gross margins by product line for unexpected fluctuations indicating misstatement

### Rights and Obligations
- Review contracts to verify the entity is the principal (not agent) for recorded revenue
- Examine consignment arrangements for proper revenue timing
- Verify bill-and-hold arrangements meet the criteria for revenue recognition
- Review licensing agreements for proper classification of licensing vs. service revenue

### Presentation and Disclosure
- Verify proper disaggregation of revenue by type, geography, and timing (ASC 606)
- Review disclosures for significant judgments in revenue recognition
- Ensure contract assets and liabilities are separately presented
- Verify disclosure of remaining performance obligations

## Debt (Notes Payable, Long-Term Debt)

### Existence / Occurrence
- Send direct confirmations to all lenders for outstanding balances, terms, and collateral
- Inspect loan agreements, promissory notes, and amendments
- Verify debt proceeds were received by inspecting bank statements
- Trace debt payments to bank statements and amortization schedules

### Completeness
- Inquire of management about all borrowing arrangements (formal and informal)
- Review board minutes for authorization of new borrowings
- Search for unrecorded debt by analyzing interest expense relative to recorded debt
- Confirm no additional borrowings exist through lender confirmations
- Review subsequent period bank statements for new loan proceeds

### Valuation / Accuracy
- Recalculate interest expense based on outstanding balances and stated rates
- Verify proper amortization of debt issuance costs and discounts/premiums
- Recalculate the current portion of long-term debt per amortization schedules
- Verify proper accounting for debt modifications and extinguishments
- Test covenant compliance calculations

### Rights and Obligations
- Identify all collateral pledged and verify against loan agreements
- Review for cross-default provisions and cross-collateralization
- Verify guarantees provided by or to the entity
- Assess whether covenant violations trigger reclassification to current

### Presentation and Disclosure
- Verify proper split between current and non-current portions
- Review disclosures for interest rates, maturity dates, and collateral
- Ensure covenant violations and waivers are disclosed
- Verify disclosure of aggregate maturities for the next five years
- Review related party debt disclosures

## Equity

### Existence / Occurrence
- Inspect corporate charter and amendments for authorized share classes and amounts
- Verify outstanding shares per the stock ledger or transfer agent confirmation
- Trace dividends declared to board minutes and authorization
- Verify treasury stock transactions with broker statements and board authorization

### Completeness
- Reconcile beginning to ending equity balances (contributions, net income, distributions, other)
- Verify all equity transactions are recorded (stock issuances, repurchases, conversions)
- Review for unrecorded equity compensation (stock options, restricted stock)
- Confirm with transfer agent that all share activity has been provided

### Valuation / Accuracy
- Verify stock-based compensation expense calculation (Black-Scholes or other model inputs)
- Recalculate earnings per share (basic and diluted)
- Test the computation of accumulated other comprehensive income
- Verify retained earnings roll-forward (beginning + net income - dividends = ending)
- Test partner capital account allocations per partnership agreement

### Rights and Obligations
- Review shareholder agreements for restrictions on equity
- Verify redemption features of preferred stock (classify as liability if mandatory)
- Review for conversion features requiring separate accounting
- Assess whether distributions are in compliance with state law and agreements

### Presentation and Disclosure
- Verify proper presentation of each equity component
- Ensure disclosure of authorized, issued, and outstanding shares by class
- Review disclosures for stock-based compensation (method, assumptions, expense)
- Verify disclosure of restrictions on retained earnings or distributions
- Ensure EPS is presented on the face of the income statement (public companies)
