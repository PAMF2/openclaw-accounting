# Variance Explanation Templates

Standard language for common financial variances in month-end reports. Each template includes a brief explanation paragraph suitable for client-facing financial summaries.

---

## Revenue Variances

### 1. Seasonal Revenue Increase
**Trigger**: Revenue up >15% MoM during known seasonal peak

> Revenue increased $[AMOUNT] ([PERCENT]%) compared to the prior month, consistent with the typical seasonal uptick we see during [SEASON/MONTH]. This is in line with the same period last year, when revenue similarly increased by [PRIOR_YEAR_PERCENT]%. No action is required; we will continue to monitor against the annual budget.

### 2. Seasonal Revenue Decline
**Trigger**: Revenue down >15% MoM during known seasonal trough

> Revenue declined $[AMOUNT] ([PERCENT]%) from the prior month. This seasonal softness is expected during [SEASON/MONTH] and is consistent with historical patterns. Prior year revenue for the same period was $[PRIOR_YEAR_AMOUNT]. Cash reserves remain adequate to cover operations through the slower period.

### 3. New Client / Contract Revenue Spike
**Trigger**: Revenue up >20% MoM with identifiable new revenue source

> Revenue increased $[AMOUNT] ([PERCENT]%) month-over-month, primarily driven by the onboarding of [CLIENT/CONTRACT_NAME], which contributed $[NEW_REVENUE] in its first [full/partial] month. Excluding this new revenue source, organic revenue [grew/declined] [PERCENT]% on a comparable basis. We expect this client to contribute approximately $[MONTHLY_AMOUNT] per month going forward.

### 4. Client Loss / Contract Completion
**Trigger**: Revenue down >10% MoM with identifiable lost revenue source

> Revenue decreased $[AMOUNT] ([PERCENT]%) compared to the prior month, primarily due to the [loss of/completion of contract with] [CLIENT_NAME], which had been contributing approximately $[LOST_AMOUNT] per month. Management is [actively pursuing replacement revenue / aware of this expected decline as the contract was scheduled to end]. The remaining client base generated $[REMAINING_REVENUE], which was [flat/up/down] [PERCENT]% excluding this impact.

### 5. Pricing Change Impact
**Trigger**: Revenue change coincides with known price adjustment

> Revenue [increased/decreased] $[AMOUNT] ([PERCENT]%) month-over-month. Approximately $[PRICE_IMPACT] of this variance is attributable to the price [increase/decrease] that took effect on [DATE]. Unit volume was [flat/up/down] [PERCENT]% on a like-for-like basis. We will continue monitoring customer retention following the pricing change.

### 6. One-Time Revenue Event
**Trigger**: Revenue spike with non-recurring source identified

> Revenue includes a one-time [payment/settlement/insurance proceeds/asset sale] of $[AMOUNT] from [SOURCE]. Excluding this non-recurring item, normalized revenue was $[NORMALIZED_AMOUNT], which represents a [PERCENT]% [increase/decrease] from the prior month. This item should not be included in run-rate projections.

## Payroll and Labor Variances

### 7. Payroll Spike - Bonus / Commission Payout
**Trigger**: Payroll up >15% MoM with bonus/commission identified

> Total payroll expense increased $[AMOUNT] ([PERCENT]%) over the prior month. This increase is attributable to [annual bonuses / quarterly commissions / performance incentives] totaling $[BONUS_AMOUNT] paid during [MONTH]. Base salaries and wages were $[BASE_AMOUNT], which was [flat/up/down] [PERCENT]% on a normalized basis. This is a [recurring quarterly / annual one-time] event.

### 8. New Hire Impact
**Trigger**: Payroll up with known headcount change

> Payroll increased $[AMOUNT] ([PERCENT]%) compared to the prior month due to [NUMBER] new [hire(s)/position(s)] added during [MONTH]. The new [role(s)] of [TITLE(S)] [was/were] budgeted and [is/are] expected to add approximately $[MONTHLY_COST] per month to the ongoing payroll run rate, inclusive of benefits and employer taxes.

### 9. Overtime Spike
**Trigger**: Overtime category up >25% MoM

> Overtime expense increased $[AMOUNT] ([PERCENT]%) month-over-month, driven by [REASON: seasonal demand / project deadline / staff shortage / special event]. Management [has approved this as a temporary measure / is addressing the underlying cause by PLAN]. We expect overtime to normalize to approximately $[NORMAL_AMOUNT] per month by [TARGET_MONTH].

### 10. Payroll Tax True-Up
**Trigger**: Unusual payroll tax amount (annual reset, rate change, audit)

> Payroll taxes [increased/decreased] by $[AMOUNT] compared to the prior month. This is due to [the annual reset of FICA wage bases in January / a state unemployment rate adjustment effective DATE / a retroactive assessment from AGENCY]. This [is/is not] expected to continue at this level. The annualized impact is approximately $[ANNUAL_IMPACT].

## Operating Expense Variances

### 11. Insurance Premium Renewal
**Trigger**: Insurance expense spikes at renewal period

> Insurance expense increased $[AMOUNT] ([PERCENT]%) due to the annual policy renewal effective [DATE]. The [general liability / workers comp / health / property] premium increased from $[OLD_PREMIUM] to $[NEW_PREMIUM] [annually/monthly]. The carrier cited [rising claims costs / industry risk factors / increased property values] as the primary driver. [Management obtained competitive quotes and this was the most favorable option / We recommend obtaining competitive quotes at next renewal.]

### 12. Software / SaaS Subscription Change
**Trigger**: Technology spend up >20% MoM

> Technology and software expenses increased $[AMOUNT] ([PERCENT]%) compared to the prior month. This is primarily due to [the addition of NEW_TOOL at $COST/mo / an annual billing for TOOL_NAME of $ANNUAL_AMOUNT / a plan upgrade for TOOL_NAME from TIER to TIER]. On a monthly normalized basis, ongoing technology spend is approximately $[MONTHLY_RUN_RATE]. [This was approved as part of the technology budget / Management should review for potential redundancies with existing tools.]

### 13. One-Time Professional Fee
**Trigger**: Professional fees spike with identifiable engagement

> Professional fees increased $[AMOUNT] ([PERCENT]%) over the prior month due to [legal fees related to MATTER / consulting engagement for PROJECT / accounting fees for YEAR-END/AUDIT]. This is a non-recurring cost that [has been fully paid / will continue for an estimated DURATION at approximately $MONTHLY_AMOUNT]. Excluding this item, recurring professional fees were $[RECURRING_AMOUNT].

### 14. Rent / Occupancy Change
**Trigger**: Rent expense changes by >5%

> Occupancy costs [increased/decreased] $[AMOUNT] ([PERCENT]%) due to [the annual rent escalation per the lease agreement / relocation to the new office at ADDRESS / the addition of warehouse space / lease renegotiation]. The new monthly rent obligation is $[NEW_RENT] compared to the prior $[OLD_RENT]. The current lease runs through [EXPIRATION_DATE]. [CAM charges also adjusted by $AMOUNT.]

### 15. Repair / Maintenance Spike
**Trigger**: R&M expense >2x monthly average

> Repairs and maintenance expense increased $[AMOUNT] ([PERCENT]%) compared to the monthly average. This was driven by [SPECIFIC_REPAIR: HVAC replacement / roof repair / equipment overhaul / plumbing emergency]. This is a non-recurring event. [The cost has been expensed as a repair / A portion ($AMOUNT) has been capitalized as it extends the useful life of the asset.] Management should consider whether a maintenance reserve should be established.

## Cost of Goods Sold Variances

### 16. Material Cost Increase
**Trigger**: COGS materials up >10% without proportional revenue increase

> Cost of goods sold increased $[AMOUNT] ([PERCENT]%) while revenue [increased only PERCENT% / remained flat / decreased]. The gross margin declined from [OLD_MARGIN]% to [NEW_MARGIN]%. This is primarily attributable to [supplier price increases on KEY_MATERIAL / supply chain disruptions / commodity price increases in COMMODITY]. Management [has locked in pricing through DATE / is exploring alternative suppliers / plans to pass through a price increase of PERCENT% effective DATE].

### 17. Improved Gross Margin
**Trigger**: Gross margin improves >2 points MoM

> Gross margin improved from [OLD_MARGIN]% to [NEW_MARGIN]%, a [POINTS] basis-point improvement. Contributing factors include [negotiated supplier discounts / improved production efficiency / favorable product mix shift toward higher-margin items / price increases taking effect]. This [is/is not] expected to be sustainable at current levels. If maintained, the annualized profit impact is approximately $[ANNUAL_IMPACT].

### 18. Inventory Write-Down
**Trigger**: Inventory adjustment or write-down recorded

> Cost of goods sold includes a $[AMOUNT] inventory [write-down / adjustment / shrinkage charge] recorded during the period. This relates to [obsolete product identified during physical count / damaged goods from INCIDENT / cycle count adjustment reconciling book to physical]. The remaining inventory balance of $[REMAINING_INVENTORY] has been reviewed and is believed to be fairly stated.

## Other Variances

### 19. Depreciation / Amortization Change
**Trigger**: D&A changes by >10% MoM

> Depreciation and amortization [increased/decreased] $[AMOUNT] ([PERCENT]%) due to [new assets placed in service during the period totaling $ASSET_COST / an asset becoming fully depreciated / the disposal of ASSET_NAME / a change in estimated useful life]. The current monthly depreciation run rate is $[MONTHLY_DA]. [Section 179 / bonus depreciation] was [elected/not elected] for the new assets.

### 20. Interest Expense Change
**Trigger**: Interest expense changes by >15% MoM

> Interest expense [increased/decreased] $[AMOUNT] ([PERCENT]%) compared to the prior month. This is due to [a new loan of $PRINCIPAL at RATE% drawn on DATE / the payoff of the LOAN_NAME / variable rate adjustment on the line of credit from OLD_RATE% to NEW_RATE% / increased utilization of the revolving credit line]. The current outstanding debt balance is $[TOTAL_DEBT] with a weighted average interest rate of [WAVG_RATE]%.

### 21. Tax Provision Adjustment
**Trigger**: Income tax expense changes significantly

> The income tax [provision/benefit] for the period is $[AMOUNT], representing an effective tax rate of [RATE]% compared to [PRIOR_RATE]% in the prior period. The change is due to [increased/decreased pre-tax income / state apportionment changes / R&D tax credits of $CREDIT / prior period true-up of $AMOUNT / changes in non-deductible expenses]. The estimated annual effective tax rate is [ANNUAL_RATE]%.

### 22. Bad Debt / Write-Off
**Trigger**: Bad debt expense recorded

> Bad debt expense of $[AMOUNT] was recorded during the period related to [CUSTOMER_NAME / aged receivables over DAYS days / a specific customer bankruptcy or dispute]. The allowance for doubtful accounts balance is now $[ALLOWANCE], representing [PERCENT]% of total accounts receivable. Accounts receivable aging: current $[CURRENT], 31-60 days $[31_60], 61-90 days $[61_90], over 90 days $[OVER_90]. [Collection efforts are ongoing / The amount has been turned over to a collection agency.]

### 23. Foreign Exchange Impact
**Trigger**: FX gains or losses recorded

> A foreign exchange [gain/loss] of $[AMOUNT] was recorded during the period due to [fluctuations in the USD/CURRENCY exchange rate / settlement of foreign-denominated invoices / remeasurement of foreign-currency-denominated assets]. The [CURRENCY] [strengthened/weakened] from [OLD_RATE] to [NEW_RATE] during the period. [Management is evaluating hedging strategies / The company maintains a natural hedge through matched foreign revenue and expenses.]

### 24. Extraordinary / Non-Recurring Item
**Trigger**: Any material unusual item

> The financial statements include a non-recurring [charge/gain] of $[AMOUNT] related to [DESCRIPTION: litigation settlement / insurance recovery / asset impairment / restructuring costs / natural disaster recovery]. Excluding this item, normalized operating income was $[NORMALIZED_OI], representing a [PERCENT]% [increase/decrease] from the prior month. This item [has been / should be] excluded from management's forecasting and budgeting models.

### 25. Budget vs. Actual Variance
**Trigger**: Any line item >10% over budget

> [LINE_ITEM] was $[ACTUAL] for the period versus a budget of $[BUDGET], an unfavorable variance of $[VARIANCE] ([PERCENT]%). Year-to-date, [LINE_ITEM] is [OVER/UNDER] budget by $[YTD_VARIANCE] ([YTD_PERCENT]%). The primary driver is [REASON]. [This is expected to self-correct in coming months as EXPLANATION / Management should consider a budget revision to reflect current run rates / Corrective action is being taken to bring spending back in line.]
