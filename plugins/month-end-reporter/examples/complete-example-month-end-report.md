### Executive Summary

March was a strong month for Cedar & Stone, with total revenue of **$271,430**, up **23.5%** from February. This increase was driven by seasonal traffic improvements, a catering event ($12,500), and strong bar sales during the March Madness period. Net operating income was **$37,125** (13.7% margin), up from $20,075 (9.1%) in February.

Key metrics are within or better than industry benchmarks, with the exception of overtime spending, which warrants attention.

**Labor Commentary:**
Total payroll expense increased $10,240 (11.7%) over the prior month, driven primarily by overtime, which more than doubled from $4,100 to $8,750. Overtime as a percentage of base wages increased from 7.0% to 14.0%, which is above the 10% target.

Management should evaluate whether the overtime spike was due to (a) the catering event requiring additional staffing hours, (b) March Madness extended service hours, or (c) scheduling inefficiencies. If driven by temporary events, overtime should normalize in April. If structural, consider hiring an additional part-time staff member, which would likely be more cost-effective than sustained overtime at 1.5x rates.

**Prime cost of 65.8% is slightly above the 65% upper target.** Labor is the driver; COGS is within range. Recommend targeting 34% labor in April to bring prime cost within the benchmark range.

### Advisory Notes

1. **Overtime Management**: Overtime spending doubled in March. While some is attributable to the catering event and extended sports-viewing hours, the 14% overtime-to-base ratio exceeds best practice. Recommend reviewing scheduling practices and evaluating whether adding a part-time line cook or server would be more cost-effective than sustained overtime. Estimated savings: $1,500-$2,500/month if overtime returns to the 7% target.

2. **Catering Revenue Opportunity**: The $12,500 catering event achieved a 65% gross margin. With two additional events reportedly booked for Q2, this could represent a meaningful revenue diversification channel. Recommend establishing a separate catering P&L to track profitability and inform pricing decisions.

3. **S-Corp Officer Compensation**: Officer compensation of $10,000/month ($120,000 annualized) appears reasonable for a restaurant grossing approximately $3M annually. No adjustment recommended at this time.

4. **Cash Position**: With net operating income of $37,125, the HVAC repair of $7,420, and the $5,000 estimated tax payment (not on P&L), net cash generation was approximately $24,700 for the month. Recommend maintaining a minimum of 2-3 months operating expenses ($265,000-$400,000) in reserves.

5. **Line of Credit**: Interest expense of $380 indicates an approximate outstanding balance of $38,000-$45,000 on the line of credit. If cash flow permits, consider paying down the LOC during the stronger spring/summer revenue months to reduce interest expense.
