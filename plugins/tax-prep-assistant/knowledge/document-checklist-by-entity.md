# Tax Preparation Document Checklists by Entity Type

---

## Individual (Form 1040)

### Personal Information
1. Prior year tax return (federal and all state returns)
2. Social Security numbers for taxpayer, spouse, and all dependents
3. Dates of birth for all household members
4. Government-issued photo ID for taxpayer and spouse
5. IP PIN (Identity Protection PIN) if issued by IRS
6. Bank routing and account numbers for direct deposit/payment
7. Estimated tax payment dates and amounts (federal and state)
8. Any IRS or state correspondence received during the year

### Income Documents
9. W-2 forms from all employers (taxpayer and spouse)
10. 1099-NEC forms for freelance or independent contractor income
11. 1099-MISC for royalties, rents, prizes, and other miscellaneous income
12. 1099-INT for interest income from banks and financial institutions
13. 1099-DIV for dividend income and capital gain distributions
14. 1099-B for brokerage transactions (stock sales, crypto, etc.)
15. 1099-R for retirement distributions (IRA, 401k, pension)
16. 1099-G for state tax refunds and unemployment compensation
17. 1099-S for real estate sale proceeds
18. 1099-C for cancellation of debt income
19. 1099-SA / 1099-Q for HSA / education savings distributions
20. SSA-1099 for Social Security benefits received
21. Schedule K-1 (Form 1065) from partnership investments
22. Schedule K-1 (Form 1120-S) from S-Corporation ownership
23. Schedule K-1 (Form 1041) from trust or estate income
24. Rental property income and expense summary (each property separately)
25. Alimony received (for divorce agreements executed before 2019)

### Deduction and Credit Documents
26. Mortgage interest statement (Form 1098) for all properties
27. Property tax bills paid during the year (primary residence, investment properties)
28. State and local income/sales tax paid (if itemizing)
29. Charitable contribution receipts and acknowledgment letters (cash and non-cash)
30. Medical and dental expense receipts (if expecting to exceed 7.5% AGI)
31. Student loan interest statement (Form 1098-E)
32. Tuition statement (Form 1098-T) for education credits
33. Childcare provider name, address, EIN/SSN, and amounts paid
34. Adoption expense receipts and documentation
35. Educator classroom expense receipts (K-12 teachers)
36. Health insurance Form 1095-A (Marketplace), 1095-B, or 1095-C
37. HSA contribution records (Form 5498-SA) and distribution records
38. IRA contribution records (Form 5498) - traditional and Roth
39. Energy-efficient home improvement receipts (solar, windows, heat pump)
40. Electric vehicle purchase documentation (VIN, MSRP, date, dealer report)

### Business / Self-Employment (Schedule C)
41. Profit and loss statement or income/expense summary
42. Business use of home measurements (office square footage, total home square footage)
43. Vehicle mileage log (total miles, business miles, commute miles)
44. Business asset purchase records (date, cost, description)
45. 1099-K forms from payment processors (Stripe, PayPal, Square)
46. Business license and registration documents
47. Self-employed health insurance premium statements

### Investment / Capital Gains
48. Cost basis documentation for sold assets (purchase dates, prices)
49. Cryptocurrency transaction history (all exchanges and wallets)
50. Installment sale agreements and payment records
51. Like-kind exchange (1031) documentation
52. Qualified Opportunity Zone investment records

## S-Corporation (Form 1120-S)

### Corporate Information
1. Prior year Form 1120-S and all K-1s issued
2. EIN (Employer Identification Number) confirmation
3. Articles of Incorporation and S-election letter (Form 2553 acceptance)
4. Corporate bylaws and shareholder agreement
5. State registration and franchise tax filings
6. Any IRS or state correspondence received during the year
7. Minutes of shareholder meetings documenting major decisions
8. Ownership percentages and any changes during the year

### Financial Statements
9. Year-end Balance Sheet (assets, liabilities, equity)
10. Profit and Loss Statement (income statement) for the full year
11. Trial Balance from accounting system (QuickBooks, Xero, etc.)
12. General Ledger detail for all accounts
13. Bank reconciliations for all accounts (December)
14. Credit card statements and reconciliations (December)
15. Accounts Receivable aging report (year-end)
16. Accounts Payable aging report (year-end)
17. Loan amortization schedules for all outstanding debt

### Income Documentation
18. Revenue detail by source / customer (for multi-state apportionment)
19. 1099-K forms received from payment processors
20. 1099-INT and 1099-DIV for corporate investment accounts
21. Gain/loss on asset dispositions (date acquired, date sold, proceeds, basis)
22. Rental income and expense detail (if applicable)

### Expense Documentation
23. Officer compensation detail (W-2 amounts, benefits, reimbursements)
24. Payroll tax returns filed (Forms 941, 940, state returns)
25. 1099-NEC forms issued to contractors (copies of all filed)
26. Retirement plan contribution records (SEP, SIMPLE, 401k)
27. Health insurance premiums paid for shareholders (>2% owners)
28. Vehicle expenses and mileage logs for officer-used vehicles
29. Depreciation schedule and new asset additions (date, cost, description)
30. Section 179 and bonus depreciation elections
31. Charitable contribution receipts
32. Meals and entertainment log with business purpose documentation
33. Travel expense detail with business purpose
34. Bad debt write-offs with documentation of collection efforts
35. Research and development expense detail (for R&D credit)

### Shareholder Information
36. Beginning-of-year shareholder basis schedules (each shareholder)
37. Shareholder loan documentation (promissory notes, repayment records)
38. Distribution register (dates, amounts, recipients)
39. Capital contribution records
40. Shareholder personal information for K-1 preparation (SSN, address)

### State and Compliance
41. Multi-state revenue allocation detail (by state)
42. State franchise tax / annual report filings
43. Sales tax returns filed (if applicable)
44. Property tax statements
45. Business licenses and permits in each operating state

## C-Corporation (Form 1120)

### Corporate Information
1. Prior year Form 1120 and supporting schedules
2. EIN confirmation letter
3. Articles of Incorporation and amendments
4. Corporate bylaws and board resolutions
5. Shareholder register (names, shares, percentages)
6. Any IRS or state correspondence received during the year
7. Board meeting minutes documenting major transactions
8. Intercompany transaction documentation (if part of affiliated group)

### Financial Statements
9. Audited or reviewed financial statements (if available)
10. Year-end Balance Sheet
11. Income Statement for the full year
12. Statement of Cash Flows
13. Trial Balance and General Ledger detail
14. Bank reconciliations for all accounts (year-end)
15. Investment account statements (year-end)
16. Loan agreements and amortization schedules
17. Lease agreements and schedules (operating and finance)
18. Deferred revenue / unearned income schedules

### Income Documentation
19. Revenue by product line / segment / geography
20. 1099-K forms received
21. 1099-INT, 1099-DIV, 1099-B for corporate investments
22. Gain/loss on asset sales (date, proceeds, adjusted basis)
23. Foreign source income detail (for Form 1118 / 5471)
24. Related party transaction detail (intercompany pricing, loans)
25. Tax-exempt income detail (municipal bonds, etc.)

### Expense Documentation
26. Officer and director compensation detail (all forms: salary, bonus, equity)
27. Payroll returns filed (941, 940, W-3 summary)
28. All 1099-NEC forms issued
29. Retirement plan contributions and administration costs
30. Employee benefit plan costs (health, dental, vision, life, disability)
31. Depreciation schedule - book and tax (MACRS schedules)
32. New asset additions with placed-in-service dates and costs
33. Asset dispositions (date, original cost, accumulated depreciation, proceeds)
34. Research and development expenses (qualified research expenditures)
35. Charitable contributions with donee acknowledgment letters
36. Advertising and marketing expense detail
37. Legal and professional fees detail (by matter/engagement)
38. Insurance premium detail by policy type
39. Bad debt write-offs and reserve methodology

### Tax-Specific Items
40. Estimated tax payment dates and amounts (federal Form 1120-W, state)
41. Prior year NOL carryforward schedule
42. Section 163(j) business interest limitation computation (if applicable)
43. Book-to-tax difference schedule (M-1 or M-3 adjustments)
44. Schedule UTP (Uncertain Tax Position) documentation
45. Transfer pricing documentation (if international operations)
46. State apportionment factors (sales, payroll, property by state)
47. Foreign tax credit documentation (Form 1118)
48. E&P (Earnings and Profits) computation from prior years
49. Dividend payment records (dates, amounts, recipients)
50. Stock issuance and redemption records

## Partnership (Form 1065)

### Partnership Information
1. Prior year Form 1065 and all K-1s issued
2. EIN confirmation letter
3. Partnership Agreement (current version with all amendments)
4. Capital account reconciliation (beginning to ending, each partner)
5. Any changes in partnership interests during the year (admissions, withdrawals)
6. Section 754 election status and any basis adjustments
7. IRS or state correspondence received during the year
8. Partnership meeting minutes documenting significant decisions

### Financial Statements
9. Year-end Balance Sheet (tax basis and book basis if different)
10. Profit and Loss Statement for the full year
11. Trial Balance and General Ledger detail
12. Bank reconciliations for all accounts (year-end)
13. Credit card reconciliations (year-end)
14. Accounts Receivable aging
15. Accounts Payable aging
16. Loan documentation and amortization schedules
17. Guaranteed payment detail (amount, recipient, dates)

### Income Documentation
18. Revenue detail by source and type
19. 1099-K forms received from payment processors
20. 1099-INT and 1099-DIV for partnership investment accounts
21. Gain/loss on property sales (ordinary vs. capital, Section 1231)
22. Rental income and expense detail (each property)
23. Royalty income and expense detail
24. Foreign source income detail
25. Tax-exempt income (municipal bond interest, etc.)

### Expense Documentation
26. Guaranteed payments to partners (treated as SE income to partner)
27. Payroll returns if partnership has employees (941, 940)
28. 1099-NEC forms issued to contractors
29. Depreciation schedule and new asset additions
30. Section 179 elections and bonus depreciation
31. Retirement plan contributions (for partner and employee plans)
32. Health insurance premiums paid for partners
33. Vehicle expense detail and mileage logs
34. Travel and meal expenses with business purpose
35. Charitable contributions
36. Bad debt write-offs
37. Research and development expenses

### Partner Information
38. Partner names, SSNs/EINs, addresses (for K-1 preparation)
39. Profit-sharing, loss-sharing, and capital-sharing percentages
40. Partner contribution records (cash and property) with dates and values
41. Partner distribution records (cash and property) with dates and values
42. Partner loan documentation (to and from partnership)
43. Partner at-risk basis calculations
44. Partner passive activity information (material participation documentation)
45. Section 704(b) capital account maintenance records
46. Hot asset (Section 751) analysis for any partnership interest transfers
47. Partner's outside basis schedules (prior year ending balances)

### Special Items
48. Section 754 step-up/step-down computations (if election in effect)
49. Qualified Opportunity Zone investment documentation
50. Multi-state nexus and filing requirements analysis
51. Foreign partnership reporting (Forms 8865, FBAR)
52. Partnership audit regime election (BBA provisions)
53. Technical termination analysis (if >50% interest changed)
54. Debt allocation schedules (recourse vs. nonrecourse, per partner)
55. Self-employment income allocation detail
